# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Vayyl serves privacy-conscious Stellar builders, protocol teams, treasury operators, traders, and early technical users who need to move value or hold positions without broadcasting amounts, counterparties, or strategy. They are likely evaluating whether the protocol is credible enough to try on testnet, integrate with, or keep watching.

## Product Purpose

Vayyl is a confidential settlement protocol for Stellar/Soroban. It uses shielded pools, commitments, nullifiers, Circom/Groth16 proofs, Poseidon2 hashing, and native Soroban BN254 verification so payments, positions, orders, and agent settlements can prove correctness without exposing sensitive details. Success for the landing page means a technical visitor understands what Vayyl is, why privacy matters on public ledgers, how the mechanism works, and what action to take next.

## Brand Personality

Discreet, technical, and decisive. Vayyl should feel like serious privacy infrastructure: calm enough for trust, sharp enough for cryptography, and specific enough to avoid generic crypto marketing.

## Anti-references

- Copied SaaS landing-page templates with interchangeable hero copy and icon-card grids.
- Generic crypto neon, purple gradients, decorative glow orbs, and glassmorphism by default.
- Privacy theater: vague claims about secrecy without showing the proof path.
- Overclaiming beyond current testnet/project status.
- Editorial affectation that makes the protocol feel like a template rather than infrastructure.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the mechanism. Privacy is credible only when the page shows how commitments, nullifiers, pools, and verifier calls fit together.
- Show constraints plainly. Testnet status, custody boundaries, and proof assumptions should increase trust, not hide in footnotes.
- Make private positions the sharper story. Payments are the foundation; strategy privacy is the differentiated promise.
- Use fewer, stronger sections. Every section should either clarify the product, prove credibility, or move the visitor toward the app/docs.
- Distinctiveness beats template polish. The page should feel built for Vayyl, not adapted from a generic Web3 design kit.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve reduced-motion handling for GSAP and scroll-driven effects. Keep keyboard navigation and focus visibility intact. Do not gate essential content behind animations that can fail, pause, or remain invisible in reduced-motion/headless contexts.
